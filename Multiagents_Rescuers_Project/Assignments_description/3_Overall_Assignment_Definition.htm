Practical work of IMAS

Practice goal

The main goal is to put in practice the basic concepts of agent technology using a real framework (JADE).

Practice description

You have to design and develop a decision making system for disaster situations based on a multi-agent system in groups of four or five students.

This exercise aims to simulate how rescue entities react to injured people in the mountain. There are two kinds of entities: (i) rural agents, that walk up to the injured people to help them in situ, and (ii) helicopters that bring injured people from the mountain to a hospital.

To make the problem finite, we locate injured trekking people in a hilly area, seen as a grid of variable size, where a limited number of elements and allowed interactions appear. The main elements to consider are the following:

  • Rural agents. Rural agents are specialized people in rescuing in mountain, and have the shared responsibility of taking care of injured people. They travel through existing paths. When idle, they have to be in mountain huts to prevent getting frozen.

  • Hospitals. There are few hospitals near mountains to take care of injured inhabitants. Injured people are brought to the hospital when they are gravily injured. Hospitals heal perfectly injured people, so that hospitals behave as if their capacity is infinite. There are few hospitals in the map.

  • Helicopters. These vehicles have a maximum capacity for injured people. They travel straight from hospitals to near the injured people. Helicopters when idle have to be stopeed at corresponding hospitals to reduce cost. Helicopters can appear in the same cell in any number, except in the following cases:

    • There can be only a helicopter rescuing people in a given cell. Other helicopters can appear moving in the same cell, though.

    • There can be only a helicopter landing on to a hospital, or taking off from a hospital. There is only a landing pad per hospital. However, there can be any number of stopped helicopters in a hospital.

  • Mountaneers. They are people doing trekking through the mountain. They just get visible on the map when they get injured. Once they are injured, they die if they are not in contact with rural agents or helicopters in at maximum of 20 steps.

  • Paths. Grid cells through which rural agents and mountaneers will be moving. They are bidirectional. A path cell can contain people without any limit and just one helicopter when rescuing. 

  • Mountains. There are grid cells that represent mountains, hills and valleys.

  • Avalanches. Mountains may have snow and other elements that may block paths for a certain amount of time.

Therefore, the goal of the whole system is to try to rescue all injured people from mountains. This task should be done spending the minimum amount of resources (e.g. fuel or walking for rural agents).

Technical requirements

Your practical work has to address this problem in two phases:

  1. Initialization. This is the first phase in which your whole solution is correctly initialized, loading the initial configuration settings from a file, creating all the agents and showing them in their initial positions in the graphical interface.

  2. Simulation. Once all agents are ready, the evolution of all events will make agents move through the map simulated by steps. At the end of each step, all mobile agents know their next position in the grid.

Architecture

To address disasters in the mountain, you have to design and develop the following agents addressing the responsibilities being detailed:

  • SystemAgent. This agent is responsible for:

    1. Loading the configuration settings at the initialization phase.

    2. Spreading the necessary configuration settings and current status of the mountain to the CoordinatorAgent.

    3. Providing a GUI where to show the map grid and all its static and mobile elements.

    4. Adding new injured people in the map in a random and dynamic way. An injured person dies after 20 steps, if a rescuing element (helicopter or rural agent) does not get near it.

      1. Deciding if injured people have to appear in the next simulation step. Probability of injury.
      2. Deciding the number of injured people.
      3. Deciding where they appear: their position.
      4. Deciding the injury's severity per person according to the probability of injury: light (90%) or severe (10%). Consider also that:
        1. People with severe injury can only be healed in hospitals.
        2. People with light injury can be healed by rural agents, moving them into the mountain huts.
      5. Injured people are not agents.
    5. Adding avalanches in a random and dynamic way. Avalanches disappear automatically on up to 5 steps.
      1. Deciding if avalanches have to appear in the next simulation step. Probability of avalanches.
      2. Deciding the number of avalanches.
      3. Deciding the number of steps for making the avalanches disappear.
      4. During isolation, helicopters can only rescue injured people if necessary. When avalanche disappears, isolated and injured people become again accessible for rural agents.

  • CoordinatorAgent. This agent can communicate with the HelicopterCoordinator and the RuralAgentCoordinator, providing them the information given by the SystemAgent.

  • RuralAgentCoordinator. This agent is responsible for coordinating RuralAgents, so that they get to know where injured people are. 

  • HelicopterCoordinator. This agent helps to manage the coordination between HelicopterAgents. It also shares with HelicopterAgents where injured people exist that only helicopters can rescue.

  • HelicopterAgent. These agents are responsible for bringing up to 2 people at a time to the hospital. These people, though, can get in the helicopter at different places from the map. Injured people get in a helicopter that is placed in any cell surrounding the injured person, at a speed of a person per step. Rescuing people by a helicopter has a cost of 20/person. 

  • RuralAgent. These agents must coordinate their actions (with the support of the RuralAgentCoordinator) to reach the goal of preventing people from die. To rescue an injured person, a RuralAgent get to the same cell than the injured person, and brings that person to a mountain hut, no matter which. Rescuing people by a RuralAgent has a cost of 1/person.

Given all these details, you have to design a system that rescues all injured people with the minimum cost (1/person for rural agents, and 20/person for helicopters).

Helicopter coordination

You have to choose one of the following coordination schemes among helicopters and hospitals.

  1. Helicopters are related to a hospital, so that all loaded injured people in those ambulances are brought to the same hospital. For instance, if helicopter1 is related to hospital3, all the people loaded in helicopter1 will be brought to hospital3.

  2. Helicopters are related to the area, not to a hospital, so that injured people can be brought to any hospital.

Helicopters travel in straight line from a hospital to the injured person or people, and then in straight line to a hospital where to heal them.

Helicopters and rural agents at work

To clarify the way both helicopters and rural agents work, these are the guidelines:

  1. A rural agent goes from any mountain hut to the cell where an injured person appears. Rural agents can bring just a person at a time. Then, rural agents bring people to any mountain hut. They have to follow existing paths. Rural agents are only effective when people are lightly injured.
  2. Helicopters take injured people to hospitals in two cases:
    1. Mandatory: When people are severely injured. Rural agents cannot help them.
    2. Optionally, whenever necessary: in cases where injured people are isolated, and no rural agent can rescue them. This would be the last option to prevent people die.

If more than one helicopter is necessary to rescue injured people in the same cell, helicopters cannot appear in the same cell when rescuing. However, helicopters may appear in surrounding cells to that of the injured people.

Information flow

The information will flow as described in the next lines:

  • The SystemAgent will load the configuration settings, such as the number of agents of any kind (HelicopterAgents, RuralAgents) as well as the number of helicopters per hospital, if applicable. In addition, configuration settings will define the whole simulation properties, including the size of the grid, the number of any kind of elements, and the total number of simulation steps, among others. When this step is done, simulation can start. SystemAgent will also communicate to the CoordinatorAgent the current status dynamically, such as which elements currently exist and their specific status.

  • HelicopterCoordinator will be responsible for a good average quality of service for all helicopters.

  • HelicopterAgents and HelicopterCoordinator will implement the chosen coordination model for helicopters and they will decide the actions for each helicopter.

  • HelicopterAgents have to count the number of cells in straight line to reach the target place. Helicopters also have to find the way to the corresponding hospital. HelicopterAgents will send to the HelicopterCoordinator the number of steps taken from its current position (always in a hospital when idle) to reach the point of loading injured people, and then also the number of steps to reach the hospital.

  • The RuralAgentCoordinator will be responsible to decide the amount of RuralAgents to sent to each place with injured people. It will send a message to each RuralAgent with its new position.

  • RuralAgents have to find the fastest path to the target place with injured people. RuralAgents will send to the RuralAgentCoordinator the number of steps taken to reach each place, and RuralAgentCoordinator will send them to the SystemAgent to log them. You have to decide what to do with RuralAgents when idle, but always remain in a mountain hut. You can also decide if RuralAgents can change planning in runtime.

  • When all coordination tasks are completed and statistical information sent, both HelicopterCoordinator and RuralAgentCoordinator will send a message OK to the CoordinatorAgent, which will forward it to the SystemAgent. Then, the SystemAgent knows that it can proceed with the next simulation step.

Any protocol message not described in this document will be defined by each workgroup to make the system work as expected.

You will be better evaluated if you use at least a couple of coordination mechanisms between agents. But take into accound this: this will let you get a higher mark if and only if you give professors good reasons for that.

Statistical information

For analysis purposes, the SystemAgent will show the statistics gathered along the whole simulation process. They will show the quality of your multiagent system design, including:

  • Total cost for the rescued people.

  • Cost ratio per rescued person.

  • Number of dead people. This value is gathered by the SystemAgent when an injured person is not rescued before 20 steps.

  • Number of rescued people.

  • Ratio of rescued people.

  • Average of number of steps to take a first rescuing element (RuralAgent or HelicopterAgent) to place with injured people.

  • Percentage of people brought to mountain huts.

  • Percentage of people brought to hospitals.

In conclusion, your design should try to miminise the cost for rescuing people, the number of dead people, and the number of steps to take a first rescuing element (RuralAgent or HelicopterAgent) to a place with injured people.

Deadline and delivery

You have to deliver the solution to this exercise in groups of four or five students. To do so, you will have two evaluation opportunities. The first evaluation opportunity has these two deadlines:

  1. December 2nd, 2015, with the initialization phase developed. Mandatory delivery in all cases to pass the subject.

  2. January 13th, 2016, with the whole practical work.

The second evaluation opportunity will be later with a unique deadline:

  • February 5th, 2016, with the whole practical work.

The group coordinator has to upload a zip archive with the solution to Moodle into the corresponding task. To do so, you are allowed to upload it since the day before the deadline, except professors decide otherwise.
